Environmental Stewardship and the Bhagawad Gita

The Bhagavad Gita, though a famed scripture on war and duty, offers surprising connections to environmental stewardship. Here's how these seemingly distant concepts intertwine:


 • Dharma and Ecological Balance: The Gita heavily emphasizes Dharma, which translates to righteous conduct or fulfilling one's duty. This concept can extend to the environment. We have a duty to maintain a balance in nature, using its resources responsibly without causing harm.


 • Nature as Divine Creation: The Bhagavad Gita portrays nature as a manifestation of the divine. Chapter 10, Verse 32, states that Lord Krishna declares himself to be the "Soul of the supporter of the Universe" ([source]). This perspective fosters reverence and respect for the natural world.


 • Sustainable Living: The Gita promotes moderation and detachment from material possessions. This aligns with sustainable living principles that encourage mindful consumption and reducing our environmental footprint.


 • Stewardship through Karma Yoga: The concept of Karma Yoga emphasizes performing actions without attachment to the outcome. Applied to environmental stewardship, one can perform actions like planting trees or cleaning up pollution with a focus on fulfilling one's duty, not personal gain.


 • Maintaining Purity: The Bhagavad Gita emphasizes cleanliness (Shaucham) as a path to spiritual growth (Chapter 13, Verse 8). This concept can be interpreted as maintaining the purity of our environment as well as ourselves.

Overall, the Bhagavad Gita, while not an explicit environmental treatise, offers principles that resonate with environmental stewardship. It encourages living in harmony with nature, fulfilling our duty towards its preservation, and recognizing its interconnectedness with our own well-being.
